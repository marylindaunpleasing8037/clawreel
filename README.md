## 🛠 Using the automation pipeline

The application uses an agent-based approach to build your videos. Here is the workflow:

**Scripting**
The agent writes a script based on your topic. You choose the tone and the length of the video. Review the script in the text editor before you move to the next stage.

**Voiceover**
Choose a voice style from the list. The software creates the audio file for your video. You can listen to the audio preview before confirming.

**Visuals**
The tool selects stock clips or generates images based on your script. You can replace any clip if you want a different look.

**Review**
The software pauses the process at specific checkpoints. This allows you to check the work of the AI. You choose to approve the section, edit it, or request a new version.

**Publishing**
Once you approve every section, the software exports the final video file. You can save this file to your hard drive and upload it to your social media accounts.

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

If you encounter problems, check these solutions:

**The installer does not start**
Ensure you have administrative rights on your computer. If the file is blocked, right-click the folder and check the properties for a "Unblock" box.

**The AI does not generate a response**
Check your API key. If the key is old or incorrect, the software cannot send requests. Update your key in the settings menu.

**Videos fail to export**
Verify you have enough space on your hard drive. Large video files require several gigabytes of free disk space. Close other programs if your computer runs slowly during the export phase.

**The software stops suddenly**
This often happens if you reach your daily or monthly cost limit. Open the settings menu to view your FinOps budget settings and verify if you hit a cap.

## 📋 Tips for better videos

- Give clear, specific instructions to the AI. Instead of "make a video about dogs," use "make a 30-second educational video about the history of golden retrievers."
- Use the checkpoints. Do not rush to the final stage. Spending a moment to check the script saves time on video editing.
- Experiment with voice settings. Different projects perform better with different voice styles.
- Organize your projects by naming them clearly. Use dates or topic names to keep your history clean.
